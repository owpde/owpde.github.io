<h2>Université libre de Bruxelles</h2>

<section id="t119">

<h3>Classification of radial blow-up at the first critical exponent for the Lin-Ni-Takagi equation in a ball</h3>

<p>We investigate the behaviour of radial positive solutions to the Lin-Ni-Takagi

problem in the ball <span class="math notranslate nohighlight">\(B_R \subset \mathbb R^N\)</span>:</p>

<div class="math notranslate nohighlight">

\[\begin{split}\left \{ \begin{aligned}

- \triangle u_p + u_p &amp; = |u_p|^{p-2}u_p &amp; \textrm{ in } B_R \\

\partial_\nu u_p &amp; = 0 &amp; \textrm{ in } \partial B_R,

\end{aligned} \right.\end{split}\]</div>

<p>when <span class="math notranslate nohighlight">\(p  \to 2^* = \frac{2N}{N-2}\)</span>. We obtain a complete classification of

finite energy blowing-up solutions to this problem. In particular, we show that

if <span class="math notranslate nohighlight">\(p \geq 2^\ast\)</span>, then solutions are compact provided that <span class="math notranslate nohighlight">\(N\geq 7\)</span>. We also

give an interpretation of our results in view of the bifurcation analysis of

Bonheure, Grumiau and Troestler in Nonlinear Anal. 147 (2016).</p>

</section>
